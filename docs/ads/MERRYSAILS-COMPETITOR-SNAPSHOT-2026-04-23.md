## What Competitors Are Pushing

Dinner cruise competitors are strongest on:

- Shuttle / pickup promise.
- Turkish night show.
- Package ladders.
- Kabatas boarding clarity.
- Reviews and OTA trust.

Private yacht competitors are strongest on:

- Per-hour or 2-hour starting price.
- Crew, fuel, and insurance included.
- WhatsApp as the fast close channel.
- Event use cases such as proposal, birthday, and corporate.

Sunset competitors are often weaker on:

- Route clarity.
- Whether the product is shared or private.
- What exactly is included at the lowest price.
- Clear direct booking reason versus OTA.

## MerrySails Response

Sunset should not fight as a vague luxury product. It should fight as:

- `From EUR 34`
- `2-Hour Bosphorus Route`
- `Maiden Tower / Dolmabahce / Ortakoy / Rumeli`
- `Wine Option From EUR 40`
- `Book Direct`

Dinner should fight directly against the strongest competitor angle:

- `From EUR 30`
- `Free Central Shuttle`
- `Dinner With Turkish Show`
- `VIP Table Options`
- `Kabatas Boarding`

Private Yacht should fight on direct package clarity:

- `From EUR 280`
- `Private Bosphorus Yacht`
- `Private Yacht With Crew`
- `Proposal / Birthday / Corporate`
- `Direct Pricing`

## Price Positioning

MerrySails visible launch anchors:

- Sunset: `EUR 34 / 40`
- Dinner: `EUR 30 / 45 / 80 / 90`
- Private Yacht: `EUR 280 / 380 / 680`

Competitor context:

- Turnatour dinner positions around 40 EUR and 80 EUR package tiers.
- GBosphorus dinner positions around 45 EUR to 125 EUR depending on table and VIP tier.
- SU Yachts shows private yacht rates starting from 4,950 TL per hour, with 2-hour examples around 9,900 TL and higher.
- Bosphorus Sunset private yacht uses the same broad 280 EUR / 380 EUR package architecture, so MerrySails must win on trust, clarity, tracking, and follow-up speed.

## Campaign Implications

Sunset:

- Keep this campaign on shared sunset cruise and wine-option searches.
- Negate dinner, private yacht, proposal, wedding, birthday, and public ferry terms.
- Use route landmarks in copy because they raise quality without overpromising.

Dinner:

- Keep shuttle copy, but qualify it as central / eligible areas.
- Use `From EUR 30` against higher competitor package anchors.
- Keep `Kabatas` ad group separate because pickup / boarding queries behave differently from generic dinner cruise queries.

Private Yacht:

- Do not promise free shuttle.
- Lead with per-yacht package clarity and event use cases.
- Keep boat rental and proposal/event ad groups separate from core yacht charter.

## Tracking Implications

Keep this hierarchy:

- `purchase` = primary and bidding signal
- `phone_click` = secondary
- `whatsapp_click` = secondary
- `contact_submit_success` = secondary
- `begin_checkout` = secondary

Reason:

- Google defines primary conversion actions as the actions used in the main `Conversions` column and bidding when the goal is active.
- GA4 web key events can be imported into Google Ads after Ads and GA4 are linked.
- WhatsApp and phone are valuable sales signals, but if they become primary too early the account may optimize toward cheap clicks instead of bookings.

## First Optimization Hypotheses

- If Dinner gets the best CTR, it is likely because free central shuttle and show copy matches competitor demand.
- If Sunset gets cheaper clicks, protect it from dinner and ferry queries with negatives.
- If Private Yacht has expensive CPC but strong WhatsApp engagement, inspect Clarity before lowering bids.
- If Yacht terms include proposal / birthday, move winners into exact match quickly.
- If broad `bosphorus cruise` terms enter through phrase matching, decide whether to negate or isolate depending on product fit.
