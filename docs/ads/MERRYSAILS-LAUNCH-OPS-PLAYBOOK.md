# MerrySails — Launch Ops Playbook

**Date:** 22 April 2026
**Purpose:** Turn the MerrySails launch into a tight, trackable, upgrade-ready Google Ads operation.
**Status:** Pre-launch strategy for activation on 23 April 2026.

---

## 1. Budget Assumption

### Working assumption

- Initial launch budget pool: `3,000 TL`
- This is treated as the current test pool to validate demand, search terms, and conversion quality before scaling.

### Recommended split

- `Sunset`: `1,250 TL`
- `Dinner`: `1,250 TL`
- `Private Yacht`: `500 TL`

### Why this split

- `Sunset` and `Dinner` are the current commercial priority products.
- They are the most direct shared-ticket products and the easiest to compare against the market.
- `Private Yacht` is valuable but should start more selectively because:
  - it has a different pricing logic
  - it can attract expensive, mixed-intent traffic
  - it needs stricter keyword and query discipline

### Scaling rule

- Increase budget only after we see:
  - qualified search terms
  - stable `purchase`
  - clean landing-page fit
  - no major waste from cross-intent traffic

---

## 2. Final Campaign Structure

### Use 3 campaigns, not 2

After budget review, the better launch setup is:

1. `Search | EN | Sunset | Istanbul Presence`
2. `Search | EN | Dinner | Istanbul Presence`
3. `Search | EN | Private Yacht | Istanbul Presence`

### Why 3 is better than 2 here

- You already know Sunset and Dinner are top priority.
- Their price ladders, logistics, and intent patterns are different enough to deserve separation.
- `Dinner` can safely use `free shuttle` style messaging.
- `Sunset` cannot.
- `Private Yacht` should stay isolated because:
  - pricing is per-yacht
  - use cases are broader
  - it is easier to waste spend there if mixed with shared-cruise demand

- Use `Presence` targeting, not `Presence or interest`.
- Primary audience is people physically in Istanbul who are already close to the buying moment.

These are the most commercially useful zones because they are tourist-heavy, hotel-heavy, and operationally aligned with your products:

- `Sultanahmet`
- `Sirkeci`
- `Taksim`
- `Beyoglu`
- `Karakoy`
- `Besiktas`
- `Harbiye`
- `Fatih`

### Why Tier 1 matters

- Dinner pickup logic already fits central European-side zones.

## 5. Competitor Gaps We Can Exploit

### Gap 1: Many sunset sellers sound like private yachts

Observed issue:
- sunset SERPs often lean into private-yacht framing
- this leaves a gap for users who want a premium-feeling but shared and cheaper option

Our move:
- own `shared sunset`
- own `from EUR 34`
- own `reserve direct`

### Gap 2: Dinner competitors often push premium pricing fast

Observed issue:
- some visible competitors push higher visible price ladders
- premium package copy dominates quickly

Our move:
- lead with:
  - `From EUR 30`
  - `Free Shuttle From Central Areas`
  - `Dinner + Turkish Show`
- then upsell ladder on the landing page

### Gap 3: Yacht competitors often push generic luxury language

Observed issue:
- many yacht players sound polished but generic
- the booking intent is often broad and message clarity is weak

Our move:
- stay clear and commercial:
  - `From EUR 280`
  - `Private Bosphorus Yacht`
  - `Proposal, Birthday, Corporate`

### Gap 4: Competitors often under-explain route landmarks

Observed issue:
- some competitors sell “experience” but not enough route-specific imagery

Our move:
- use route landmarks in ads and assets where possible:
  - `Maiden's Tower`
  - `Dolmabahce Palace`
  - `Ortakoy Mosque`
  - `Rumeli Fortress`
  - `Bosphorus Bridge`

This is especially strong for:
- sunset
- yacht

## 7. Tracking And Reporting Rules

### Events that matter most for launch

- `purchase`
- `whatsapp_click`
- `phone_click`
- `contact_submit_success`
- `begin_checkout`

### Launch decision rule

- Optimize bids around `purchase`
- Read `whatsapp_click` and `phone_click` as support signals
- Do not let Google chase soft conversions instead of actual reservation submissions

### Reporting we should check every day once live

1. Campaign
2. Ad group
3. Search term
4. Device
5. CTR
6. CPC
7. `begin_checkout`
8. `purchase`
9. `phone_click`
10. `whatsapp_click`

### What “good” means in the first days

- search terms match landing-page intent
- no major sunset/dinner/yacht crossover
- clicks come from hotel / tourist / central-area demand rather than broad local noise
- purchase starts appearing even at low volume
