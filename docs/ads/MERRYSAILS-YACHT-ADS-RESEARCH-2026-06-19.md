## 4. Targeting — does the "rich hotel-district" idea work? (No — here's the fix)

**The operator's "geo-radius around Rixos Tersane / Çırağan / Four Seasons" premise is mechanically broken. Drop it.**

Three reasons it fails:
1. **Geo-radius can't say "rich" or "tourist."** Standing near a 5-star hotel is not an income signal Google exposes. A radius in "Presence" mode catches **locals, hotel staff, commuters, Turkish residents** — not the in-room UK/DE/Gulf guest ([benly.ai](https://benly.ai/learn/google-ads/google-ads-location-targeting), [growthmindedmarketing.com](https://growthmindedmarketing.com/blog/google-ads-location-targeting/)). Wealth/luxury intent comes from **audiences + keywords**, not a map pin.
2. **The true UHNW guest is unreachable by ads anyway** — 96% of ultra-luxury travelers book via agents/concierge ([ColorWhistle](https://colorwhistle.com/luxury-travel-consumer-statistics/)); the 5-stars run in-house concierge charters ([Peninsula](https://www.peninsula.com/en/istanbul/special-offers/rooms/private-yacht-experience)). You cannot buy into the concierge channel with a TL ad budget. And your €220–500 product isn't the UHNW tier anyway — your buyer is the **affluent self-booking couple/family**, who searches *pre-trip from home*.
3. **Small radius = unreliable delivery** ("ads might only show intermittently or not at all") + 15–25% of mobile traffic masks location ([benly.ai](https://benly.ai/learn/google-ads/google-ads-location-targeting)).

**The recipe that actually works:**
- **Channel: Search only.** No PMax (your KWT note + independent tests show ~35% lower ROAS, and it needs conversion volume you don't have), no Demand Gen yet ([atlasperk.com](https://atlasperk.com/guides/paid-advertising-for-travel/campaign-strategy/)).
- **Geo = SOURCE COUNTRY, not city zone.** Target UK/US (+ AR/RU later) with **"Presence or interest"** so you hit planners *at home, pre-trip*. Booking windows are lengthening (49+ days out internationally; front-load 6–12 weeks before peak) — in-destination is too late ([atlasperk.com](https://atlasperk.com/guides/paid-advertising-for-travel/campaign-strategy/)).
- **Hotel-district geo only as a small +bid modifier** (Beşiktaş/Sarıyer/Fatih waterfront), never the primary lever, and only because a guest in the Çırağan lobby searching "Istanbul private yacht" is your ideal click. Keyword-gate first, geo-boost second.
- **Audiences (observation layer, observe-then-bid):** in-market *Luxury Travel / Vacation Packages*; affinity *Boating & Sailing / Luxury Travelers* ([utopiads.com](https://utopiads.com/how-to-target-luxury-brands-on-google/), [takeflyte.com](https://www.takeflyte.com/blog/8-google-ads-audiences)).
- **Layer RLSA retargeting** on your existing site/cart visitors — given a strong organic top-funnel, this is where paid pays back best.

## 5. ROI / Budget — the math at 1–2k TL/day (honest)

**₺1–2k/day = €25–55/day ≈ €1,000–1,650/mo.** Realistic charter-term CPC band €1.5–2.5 (above the $1.53 travel avg, far below the $30–50 UHNW-jet tier; TR auctions ~32% cheaper than US) ([get-ryze.ai](https://www.get-ryze.ai/blog/google-ads-cost-benchmarks-by-industry-2026), [Bydik](https://bydik.com/average-cost-per-click-adwords/)).

| Scenario | CPC | Clicks/day @€40 | Click→booking | Bookings/mo | CPA | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Optimistic | €1.5 | 27 | 1.5% | ~12 | **€83** | Profitable IF it holds |
| Realistic | €2.0 | 20 | 0.8% | ~5 | **€240** | Marginal |
| Pessimistic (= your actual history) | €2.5 | 16 | 0.3% | ~1.5 | **€1,100** | **Loss** |

High-ticket cold click→purchase runs **0.3–1.5%** ([Firework](https://firework.com/blog/average-e-commerce-conversion-rate-for-high-ticket-sales), [Fyresite](https://www.fyresite.com/average-ecommerce-conversion-rate-for-high-ticket-sales/)). Your real history (1 ad booking / 2 months) sits at the **pessimistic floor**.

**Break-even:** at ~70–80% contribution margin on a €220–500 charter (€180–400/booking net), €1,000–1,650/mo spend needs **~4–8 ad bookings/month** to break even.
- Optimistic clears it; realistic is borderline; **your actual history is ~10× below break-even.**
- **vs the free funnel:** organic + ChatGPT drive ~21 bookings / 2 months at **CPA ≈ €0**. Ads can't beat free — they only earn their keep if **incremental + self-liquidating (CPA < margin)**.

**GO/NO-GO threshold:** Run ₺1k/day, EN-only, 30-day window. **Kill criterion: if after €600–750 spend you have <2 attributed charter bookings (CPA > ~€350), stop.** Do not scale to ₺2k until a 30-day test proves CPA < ~€250.

## 7. What the operator MUST do (before spending a lira)

1. **FIX THE BOOKING FUNNEL FIRST (highest-leverage move).** The known **/reservation dead-clicks + English-only booking UI (open P0, task #12)** will sink paid conversion. Running ads into a leaky funnel just burns the €25–55/day. *Fix #12 before/with the test — this beats any ad tweak.*
2. **Fix conversion tracking.** Your 1-of-21 gclid rate is partly mis-attribution: organic/ChatGPT bookers don't carry gclid, and if bookings close on WhatsApp you're structurally under-crediting ads. Verify **gclid + Enhanced Conversions fire on the reservation/Purchase event**; set up **WhatsApp-click + offline-conversion import** so you can read ROAS honestly. Your conversion labels already exist in Vercel (Purchase: `UNEQCIWVrqEcEJ7x2LxD`) — confirm they fire.
3. **Build the AR landing page + finish /ru** before turning on AR/RU ad groups. EN is the only market you can run cleanly today.
4. **Account hygiene:** manual CPC, PMax stays OFF, AI Max Final-URL-Expansion OFF (blog/guide pages absorb spend otherwise). Keep within your ₺2,000/day safety cap — but only fund ₺1k for this test.
5. **2SV on all MCC users** (Google Ads MFA enforcement Apr 21 2026 — refresh tokens fail otherwise).
6. **Don't over-invest the budget — invest the *angle*.** Put "from €220, whole boat, direct, no concierge/broker markup (which adds 15–30% + pier fees), TÜRSAB-A since 2001" into the **landing page + AI-readable content**, where it compounds for free in the ChatGPT/organic funnel that already converts — rather than renting it by the click.
