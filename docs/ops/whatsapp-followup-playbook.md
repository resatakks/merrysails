# WhatsApp Follow-Up Playbook — Yacht Charter & Cruise Leads

**For:** MerrySails **and** GoldenSunsetTour (same funnel, same 4–5 day sales
conversation). Copy-paste by hand on a phone — **no automation, no API.**

**Why this exists:** ~80% of sales need 5+ touches, but most reps stop after 1.
A €220–500 yacht charter or a group dinner cruise is a considered purchase — the
guest is comparing, checking dates with friends/family, and waiting on a partner.
The money is lost in the 4–5 day silent gap, not in the first reply. This deck
keeps a lead alive across that gap without ever sounding pushy.

---

## 🔑 The 3 rules (never break these)

1. **Reply in under 5 minutes** during business hours (10:00–22:00 Istanbul). The
   guest is messaging 3–4 operators at once; the fast, human reply wins the
   conversation before price is even discussed.
2. **Every message proposes ONE next step.** A question, a date to hold, a photo
   to send — never a dead-end "let me know." Give them something easy to say yes to.
3. **Never leave a lead without a next-touch date.** After every reply, decide
   *when* you'll follow up if they go quiet, and note it. A lead with no scheduled
   next touch is a lost lead.

> **Honesty guardrails:** No fake urgency ("only 1 spot left!" when it's not). No
> invented discounts. The only real scarcity is true: **one boat, one time slot** —
> a private yacht genuinely can't be double-booked, so a same-day hold is honest.
> The published midweek discount (Mon/Tue/Thu) is real — quote it, don't fabricate.

# THE CADENCE — 6 touches

Placeholders to fill: `[Name]` `[date]` `[group size]` `[yacht/cruise]` `[€price]`.
The **English** version is the default. Below touches 1, 2 and 4 there's an
**Arabic version** — that's our cheapest-converting cohort (Gulf guests), so those
three touches are the ones worth having ready in Arabic.

---

## Touch 1 — Instant acknowledgement (< 5 minutes)

**Timing:** the moment the inquiry lands.
**Goal:** win the "who replied first & like a human" race, and get the two facts
(date + group size) you need to quote — without interrogating them.

> Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out to **MerrySails** 🙂
>
> I'd love to help you plan this. Two quick things so I can send you the exact
> price and the right boat:
>
> **1.** Which date (or rough dates) are you thinking of?
> **2.** How many guests will be joining?
>
> I'll come straight back with options. We're TÜRSAB-licensed and have hosted
> 50,000+ guests on the Bosphorus since 2001, so you're in good hands.

*(GoldenSunsetTour version: swap the name, and "45,000+ guests".)*

---

## Touch 2 — The real quote (same day, right after they answer)

**Timing:** within minutes of them giving date + group size.
**Goal:** a specific, honest quote + exactly what's included + a photo/video prompt
+ a trust line. This is the message that turns an inquiry into a real conversation.

> Perfect, thank you [Name] 🙏 For **[group size] guests on [date]**, here's what
> I'd recommend:
>
> 🛥️ **[Boutique Yacht — up to 12 guests]** — **from €[220] for a 2-hour private
> charter** (the whole yacht is yours, not shared).
>
> **Included:** licensed captain + crew, fuel, soft drinks & water, light snack
> tray, Bluetooth audio. Minimum booking is 2 hours, and it's **10% off
> automatically if you go 3 hours or more** (great for sunset — you catch golden
> hour *and* the lights coming on).
>
> Here's the actual boat you'd be on 👇
> *[attach 2–3 real photos / a short video of that yacht]*
>
> We're TÜRSAB A-Group licensed (since 2001), rated **4.9/5**, and I usually reply
> here within a few minutes if you have any questions. Want me to check this exact
> **[date]** is free?

*(Dinner/sunset lead? Same shape: quote the real package, e.g. "Sunset cruise €34
per person — €30 on Mon/Tue/Thu — 2 hours from Karaköy, live guide onboard," then
the photo + trust line + a next-step question.)*

*(GoldenSunsetTour: "rated 4.76/5", Captain Yusuf's routes, 45,000+ guests.)*

---

## Touch 3 — Gentle nudge (day 1–2, if quiet)

**Timing:** ~24 hours after Touch 2 with no reply (or same evening if they went
quiet mid-chat).
**Goal:** re-open the conversation and quietly clear the #1 hesitation (price /
date flexibility / what's included) before they even raise it.

> Hi [Name], just floating this back up 🙂 No rush at all.
>
> A couple of things that often help people decide:
> • The **[date]** you mentioned is still open on my side.
> • If your group size shifts a little, the price flexes with it — I can re-quote
>   in a second.
> • Everything (captain, crew, fuel, drinks) is already in the price — no surprise
>   extras on the day.
>
> Is there anything I can clarify — the route, timing, or the boat itself?

---

## Touch 4 — Honest hold / small deposit (day 2–3)

**Timing:** ~2–3 days in, once there's genuine interest but no commitment.
**Goal:** give a *real* reason to act now. A private yacht is one boat, one slot —
that scarcity is true, so use it honestly. Offer to hold the date.

> [Name], one honest heads-up 🙂 A private yacht is literally **one boat, one time
> slot** — so once someone books **[date]**, that evening's gone for everyone else.
>
> I don't want you to miss it if it's the day you want. I'm happy to **hold [date]
> for you** while you confirm with your group — no charge to pencil it in, and a
> small refundable deposit locks it fully. Totally your call.
>
> Want me to put a soft hold on **[date]** for now?

> **Note:** Only promise a hold/deposit flow the operator can actually honour.
> Keep it refundable and low-pressure — the goal is to remove risk, not create fear.

---

## Touch 5 — Friendly check-in + easy out (day 4–5)

**Timing:** day 4–5, the edge of the gap where most leads die.
**Goal:** show you're relaxed and human. Giving them an easy exit paradoxically
re-engages the ones who felt awkward about going quiet.

> Hi [Name] 🙂 I'll stop crowding your inbox after this one.
>
> Totally fine if the plans or timing changed — happens all the time. If the
> **[date]** slipped, just tell me the new window and I'll hold a different date
> for you instead. And if now's not the moment at all, no worries at all — I'm
> here whenever it is.
>
> Should I keep **[date]** pencilled in, or look at a different day?

---

## Touch 6 — Soft close / keep-warm (optional, ~1 week)

**Timing:** ~7 days after Touch 5, if still no reply.
**Goal:** leave the door wide open on a warm note so a future message feels natural.

> Hi [Name] — no reply needed 🙂 Just closing the loop so you're not waiting on me.
>
> Whenever you're ready to sail the Bosphorus — this trip, a birthday, an
> anniversary, or friends visiting — message me here and I'll sort it out fast.
> We're on the water year-round. Have a great one! ⛵

## ⏱️ Quick-glance cadence

| Touch | When | One-line goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | < 5 min | Acknowledge + ask date & group size |
| 2 | Same day | Real quote + what's included + photo + trust |
| 3 | Day 1–2 | Nudge + clear the top hesitation |
| 4 | Day 2–3 | Honest hold / refundable deposit |
| 5 | Day 4–5 | Friendly check-in + easy out |
| 6 | ~Day 7 | Soft keep-warm close |

**After every reply:** propose one next step, and write down the next-touch date.
No lead leaves the chat without one.
